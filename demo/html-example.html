历史沿革

Chartres Cathedral, mostly constructed between 1194 and 1220, is one of the finest examples of French Gothic architecture.

"A masterpiece of the flowering of Gothic art in the 12th and 13th centuries."

— UNESCO World Heritage

查特蓝

Chartres Blue ( #1A2A6C) is a legendary shade of cobalt blue used extensively in the cathedral's stained glass.

  • Produced with cobalt oxide imported from Central Europe
  • Withstands temperatures above 1200°C
  • Retains luminosity for over 8 centuries
